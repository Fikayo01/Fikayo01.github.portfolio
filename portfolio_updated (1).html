My Story

Where Strategy Meets
Human-Centered Design

I'm Fikayo Adewumi, an instructional designer with deep experience in learning & development. My work sits at the intersection of strategy, psychology, and design — building programs grounded in how people actually learn, not how organizations assume they do.

With a background spanning emerging technologies for learning, eLearning development, facilitation, curriculum design, and LMS administration, I bring a full-spectrum perspective to every engagement. I've partnered with teams across academic space and industries to untangle complex knowledge gaps and turn them into clear, compelling learning journeys.

I believe the best learning solutions are built on genuine empathy for the learner (learner centered), rigorous performance analysis, and a design sensibility that respects people's time and intelligence.

01
Learner-First Design
Every decision starts with the person doing the learning — their context, their barriers, their goals.
02
Outcomes Over Output
I measure success by behavior change, not course completions. Design follows the data.
03
Strategic Alignment
Learning solutions are only as valuable as their connection to real business objectives.
04
Intentional Craft
Thoughtfully designed experiences signal respect for your learners' time and intelligence.
How I Work

A Rigorous, Human-Centered
Design Process

Every project follows a structured methodology — from discovery through delivery — ensuring nothing is left to chance.

01 — Discover
Needs Analysis
Stakeholder interviews, performance gap analysis, and learner audience research to define the real problem to solve.
02 — Define
Strategy & Blueprint
Learning objectives, modality selection, instructional approach, and timeline mapped against business goals.
03 — Design
Storyboard & Prototype
Content architecture, storyboarding, visual design mockups, and interactive prototypes for early feedback.
04 — Develop
Build & Iterate
Full development in Articulate, Captivate, or custom formats with iterative review cycles built in.
05 — Deliver
Launch & Evaluate
LMS deployment, pilot testing, and Kirkpatrick-model evaluation to measure real impact.

Case Study — Payment Card Security

Turning Payment Card Security Training from a Checkbox into a Conversation

A national financial services company came to me with a critical problem: their annual compliance training had a 53% completion rate, and feedback showed learners found it irrelevant and demoralizing. Regulatory pressure was mounting, and the existing approach simply wasn't working.

The Challenge
Click-through modules with no connection to real cardholder data handling situations. Learners "completing" without engaging. PCI violation risk rising.
The Solution
Scenario-based design with realistic cardholder data situations, branched consequences, and immediate feedback tied to PCI DSS requirements.
